Age Distribution of Sydney's Population


What is the data set?
Age by sex.

Why is it collected?
It is collected for census records.

Which government department would use the data set? Provide evidence.
Department of education: selecting locations for primary, secondary, and tertiary schools.
Department of health: location of hospitals.

Which private sector would use the data set? Provide evidence.
Child-care centre developers would use the data to determine optimal locations.
Age-care facility developers for selecting locations.

How does the data set change the way you think about space?
The data set shows the distribution of childrens, teens, adults, and elderly citizens across the state of NSW.
Adults between 20 to 40 years of age are more likely to live closer to the CBD for study and work, while young families or elderly citizens live further west or north of Sydney, where property prices are cheaper.

How does the data set change across Sydney?
Young and old citizens are spread evenly across the state.
20-40 year old adults live closer to the CBD, Macquarie Park, and Parramatta.
